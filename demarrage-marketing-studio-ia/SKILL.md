# Démarchage Marketing — Studio IA Pub

## Overview

This skill provides day-to-day tactics for converting prospects on LinkedIn into paying clients. It's a **execution playbook**, not strategy — use this after you've defined your market and messaging (see Stratégie-Studio-IA-Pub for that).

## LinkedIn Content Calendar

### Three Content Pillars (3 posts/week)

Use this exact schedule to build consistency and pattern-matching in your audience's brain.

#### Pillar 1: Ad Breakdown (Monday)
**Goal:** Share actionable insights. Most shareable content.

**Format:** Video (20–30s) or image carousel (3–4 slides)

**Structure:**
1. Screenshot of a live e-commerce ad (Meta Ad Library or TikTok Creative Center)
2. Hook analysis: What grabs attention in the first 2s?
3. Story structure: How does it unfold?
4. Psychology: Which pain/desire does it trigger?
5. Lesson: "This is why [insight] matters in your testing"

**Tone:** Educational, specific, not promotional.

**Example breakdown:**
```
[Screenshot of Glossier ad on TikTok]

Hook (0–2s): Close-up of skin texture, dramatic before-after cut.
Why it works: Immediate tangibility. Viewers see themselves in it.

Story (2–6s): Testimonial voiceover + product in action.
Why this order: Trust after curiosity. Hook → belief → desire.

The lesson: E-commerce creators obsess over product shots. 
Glossier leads with *problem recognition*. 
Before you shoot your next ad, ask: 
"Does my first 2 seconds make them stop and feel seen?"
```

**Cadence:** Every Monday at 9am local time (or when your audience wakes up).

**Template for file organization:**
```
content-calendar/
├─ monday-breakdowns/
│  ├─ glossier-glow.md
│  ├─ shein-urgency.md
│  └─ ...
├─ wednesday-process/
└─ friday-opinions/
```

#### Pillar 2: Making-Of / Process (Wednesday)
**Goal:** Demystify the work. Build trust through transparency.

**Format:** 
- Carousel (5–7 slides) showing workflow
- Short video (15–30s) of tool usage
- Screenshot + caption chain

**What to show:**
1. The brief: What did the client ask for?
2. Competitive research: 10 ads you analyzed, key hooks spotted
3. Scripts: 3–4 example hooks you wrote
4. Production: Uncut footage from HeyGen/Arcads
5. Post-production: CapCut edit, music, subtitles
6. The hypothesis doc: 1 page explaining your choices
7. Delivery moment: Client reaction or results snippet

**Why:** Most creators hide their process. You expose it. This kills the "magic box" perception and positions you as methodical, not mysterious.

**Tone:** Behind-the-scenes, confident, no apologies.

**Example post:**
```
Just shipped 5 variations for an e-commerce client testing on Meta.

Here's how it goes:

[Slide 1] The brief: E-commerce course, target = solopreneurs stuck at 5k/mo
[Slide 2] Competitor research: Found these 3 hooks repeating across winners
[Slide 3] My 5 hooks: Agitation, aspiration, social proof, urgency, curiosity
[Slide 4] Scripts (excerpt): "Your 9-to-5 is a lie..." + 4 others
[Slide 5] Prod (raw): HeyGen avatar, uncut, 3 min/variation
[Slide 6] Edit (final): CapCut, subtitles, beat-synced cuts
[Slide 7] Hypothesis doc: "Why hook #3 (social proof) should win"

72h start-to-finish. Not because I'm fast. 
Because the *system* is tight.

This is what you get if you work with me: 
not just videos, but a tested methodology that your team can learn from.
```

**Cadence:** Every Wednesday at 9am.

#### Pillar 3: Opinion / Hot Take (Friday)
**Goal:** Generate debate, comments, reach. Establish POV.

**Format:** Single image + text, or short video (20–30s)

**Formula:**
1. Claim a widely-held belief in e-commerce advertising
2. Flip it: "Actually, this is backwards because..."
3. Counter-intuitive insight: What do most creators get wrong?
4. Actionable lesson: How should they think instead?

**Tone:** Bold but not rude. Confident, not arrogant.

**Examples:**

```
MYTH: "Higher production quality = higher CTR"

TRUTH: A well-tested TikTok-style vertical video beats 
an expensive 4K horizontal shoot 90% of the time.

Why: Platforms reward native content. Viewers smell "ad" 
a mile away. Authenticity (perceived) > polish (actual).

For your next campaign: 
Test a rough vertical version first. 
Then spend money on the winner.
Don't reverse-engineer it.
```

```
MYTH: "My audience is too sophisticated for AI avatars"

TRUTH: Your audience doesn't care about the tool. 
They care: Does this solve my problem? Can I trust this?

I use AI in 70% of my productions. 
Tested against human talent. 
AI wins on speed (72h) and variation (5 angles, not 1).

Using AI isn't a shortcut. It's a *strategy* for testing faster.
Own it.
```

**Cadence:** Every Friday at 9am.

### Content Batching System

**Why batch:** Prevents overwhelm, maintains consistency, builds reserves.

**Weekly routine (2 hours, Friday afternoon):**
1. Record/write 3 posts (1 of each pillar type)
2. Schedule them for next week (Mon/Wed/Fri 9am)
3. Add 2–3 hooks for future breakdowns
4. Done for the week — no daily scrambling

**Tools to use:**
- **Scheduling:** LinkedIn native scheduler or Later, Buffer
- **Assets:** Canva for graphics, CapCut for video clips, OBS for screenshares
- **Tracking:** Spreadsheet with publish date, post type, engagement metrics

**Fallback content (for when you're busy):**
- Repost an old breakdown with new angle
- Ask audience: "What ad should I break down next?" (gets comments)
- Quote thread: Share a client win anonymously

## Prospect Identification & List Building

### Target Profile Criteria

Use this to quickly filter prospects. You're looking for **founders with active creative budgets**, not every e-commerce founder.

**Green lights (reach out):**
✅ Posted in last 7 days (active)
✅ Mentioned "scaling on Meta" or "TikTok ads" or "creative testing" (budget-aware)
✅ Comments section shows founder-to-founder conversation (accessible)
✅ 1k–50k followers (not mega-influencer, not unknown)
✅ Niche: e-commerce, SaaS, digital products (not B2B services)

**Red flags (skip):**
❌ No posts in 3+ months (ghost account)
❌ All posts are job postings (hiring, not scaling)
❌ Content is all educational/thought leadership, no product mention (not a founder)
❌ Comment section is all bots or promotional (engagement farmed, low quality)

### Building Your Prospect List

**System:**
1. Find 3–4 "seed" accounts: successful founders in your niche with active profiles
2. View their followers (LinkedIn shows top followers first)
3. Apply green/red light criteria
4. Record in a spreadsheet

**Spreadsheet columns:**
```
| Name | LinkedIn URL | Company | Last post | Engagement | Status | Notes |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alice Martin | linkedin.com/in/... | ShopFlow | 3 days ago | High | Target | Active scaling, Meta focus |
| Bob Chen | linkedin.com/in/... | PrintPro | 5 days ago | Medium | Warm | Engaged with 2 posts |
| Carol S | linkedin.com/in/... | StyleCo | 2w ago | Low | Cold | No engagement yet |
```

**Target: 30–40 prospects total. Engage with 20/week. Pitch 5/week.**

## Engagement & Warm Outreach Sequence

### The 4-Week Warm-Up Cycle

**Goal:** Become a familiar face before pitching. If they recognize you as a thoughtful commenter, your DM has 3x higher response rate.

#### Week 1–3: Presence Phase
```
Week 1: Follow + Comment on 1–2 recent posts
        (5 substantive comments minimum, across 3–4 people)

Week 2: Continue commenting (not daily, not spam)
        Add 1–2 new targets to list
        Like posts consistently (signals interest without noise)

Week 3: Pick 2–3 warmest targets
        Comment again on their newest post
        Gauge receptiveness from reply quality
```

**Comment guidelines:**

❌ **Bad comments** (ignored or reported as spam):
- "Nice post! Check out my service 👇"
- Generic emoji reactions
- "Interested? DM me"
- One-liners with no substance

✅ **Good comments** (get replies, build relationship):
- Ask a specific question about their process
- Share a relevant data point: "I've seen this work for [similar company]"
- Reference something they said 2 weeks ago (shows you've been following)
- Offer a micro-insight: "Have you tested [angle]? This brand had success with..."

**Example comment:**
```
You mentioned testing 3 hooks per campaign. 
I've found e-commerce creators usually test just 1–2 
and lose 50% of potential winners. 

What's your current testing rhythm? 
Are you doing this weekly or per product launch?
```

#### Week 4: Contact Phase
**Timing:** DM after 3–4 substantive comments and they've replied to at least one.

**DM template (adapt to their situation):**

```
Hi [First name],

I've been following your growth on [Meta/TikTok] — 
the creative strategy you're running is sharp.

I'm building something for founders like you: 
5 ad angle variations in 72h + competitive analysis + testing protocol.

The insight: Most creators test 1–2 angles. 
Testing 5 simultaneously divides your creative risk by 5 
for less than you'd spend on one agency video.

I'm piloting this with 2 founders at 500€ (instead of 1,200€) 
in exchange for a testimonial once we see results.

Interested in 15 min to see if this fits? No pressure—just curious.
```

**DM don'ts:**
- Don't sell in the DM. Just make them curious.
- Don't say "let me know if you're interested" — schedule the call in the message.
- Don't be salesy. You're talking founder-to-founder.

**If no reply after 5 days:**
- 1 follow-up after 7 days: "Hey [name], didn't want this to get lost. Thought of something for your last campaign—mind if we grab 15 min?"
- Stop after 2 attempts. Don't be the guy who spams.

## First Call Script & Qualification

### Pre-Call Checklist
Before the call, research:
- Their last 5 posts: What are they trying?
- Their website/product: Understand the offer
- Their ICP: Who do they sell to?
- Current budget: Rough estimate from ad library or hints in posts

### Call Structure (15 minutes)
**Goal:** Qualify + pitch a free sprint.

```
[0–2 min] Warm-up
└─ "Thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy.
    I've been following your Meta strategy — really smart.
    Why'd you go all-in on [platform] right now?"

[2–6 min] Diagnosis (Listen 80%, talk 20%)
├─ "What's your current biggest creative bottleneck?"
├─ "How often do you need new variations?"
├─ "What happens when a creative underperforms?"
└─ "Who's handling your creative now? Freelancer, in-house, you?"

[6–12 min] Pitch & Offer
└─ "Here's what I do: [30-second pitch]. 
    The reason I'm reaching out now: 
    I'm looking for 2 beta testers at 500€ 
    (normally 1,200€) in exchange for a testimonial 
    once we have results.
    
    Would it make sense to do a test sprint with me? 
    I'd deliver 5 variations in 72h, 
    you run them for a week, 
    and we hop on a call to review data. 
    Zero risk—if it doesn't help, no hard feelings."

[12–15 min] Close
├─ If yes: "Great. Let me send you a brief form. 
            Once I get your info, we kick off tomorrow."
├─ If maybe: "What would make this a yes?"
└─ If no: "No worries. Stay in touch—keep crushing it."
```

### Red Flags (Don't Work With Them)
- ❌ Budget < 2k€/mo on ads (pain point too small)
- ❌ Doesn't post publicly about their business (not a founder mindset)
- ❌ Immediately asks "Can you guarantee 20% ROAS?" (won't understand your model)
- ❌ Wants a free test first (boundary-tester)

write hypotheses, run the testing protocol, and debrief data together.

Worth 15 min to see?

## Post-Sprint Messaging

### After Sprint Delivery (Day 1)
```
Hi [Name],

Just delivered your 5 variations. 
Folder link: [WeTransfer or Notion link]

What's inside:
• 5 video variations (MP4, ready to upload)
• Hypothesis doc (why I chose each hook)
• Testing protocol (recommended budget, duration, KPI)

I'll be honest: Weeks 1–2 might feel flat. 
That's normal. Week 2–3 is when real data flows.

Let's hop on a quick call Thursday [day+7] to read the numbers together.
```

### Day 7 Debrief Prep (1 day before)
```
Looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time].

Before we jump on:
• Check your Ad Manager for these key metrics:
  - Hook rate (how many watch 3+ seconds)
  - CTR (click-through rate)
  - CPA (if you're tracking conversions)

We'll read the data together and decide:
1. Which angle to scale
2. What to test next round

See you then!
```

### Day 7 Debrief Script (On the call)
```
[Pull up their Ad Manager together]

"OK, let's read this. Which variation surprised you the most?"

[Let them answer—they'll sell themselves on the process]

"So here's what I see:
 [Hook A] crushed on CTR but lower CPA.
 [Hook B] held longest watch time.
 [Hook C] converted best.
 
 What should we test next? 
 Should we double down on C, or explore 5 new angles around that winner?"

[If they want more:] 
"Great. Let's move you to retainer. 
2 sprints/month + call after each one. 
This way you're always testing, never stuck with old creatives."
```

## Avoiding Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it fails | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Posting inconsistently | Audience doesn't see you as a real voice | Batch 3 posts on Friday, schedule for week |
| Pitching too early (day 1–2) | Looks spammy, low response | Wait for 3–4 substantive comments first |
| DM is too long | Scrolling phone, TL;DR | Keep DM under 50 words. Link to longer version if needed. |
| Following dozens of people daily | LinkedIn flags as bot activity | Max 5 new follows/week. Focus on quality. |
| Talking about your tool, not their pain | Self-centered, not client-centered | Always start with their problem: "I see you're testing..." |
| No follow-up system | Leads die in DMs | CRM spreadsheet + 3-touch rule (comment, DM, follow-up DM) |
| Accepting every call | Wastes time on wrong-fit prospects | Qualify first: "Quick question—what's your monthly ad budget?" |
