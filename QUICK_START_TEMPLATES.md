### Template B: Problem Reframe (Algorithm Diagnostic)
```
Salut [Name],

J'ai regardé tes ads actives sur Meta.
Tu tournes avec [X] créatifs depuis [Y] semaines — ta fréquence doit être autour de 5-6 là.

Ce n'est pas Meta qui marche moins bien.
C'est l'algorithme Advantage+ qui manque de créatifs à tester.

Je peux t'envoyer 5 nouveaux angles testables en 48h.

Intéressé ?
```

**Pourquoi ça marche :**
- Pose un diagnostic précis (fréquence élevée = créatifs épuisés), pas une offre de service
- Montre que tu comprends leur problème mieux qu'eux
- Tu ne vends pas des vidéos — tu vends de l'oxygène pour l'algorithme

---

**Contexte technique à connaître pour ce pitch :**
- Meta Advantage+ recommande 10-50 créatifs pour optimiser correctement
- Sous 5 créatifs = algorithme sous-optimisé = ROAS qui chute
- Depuis iOS 14.5 (2021), Meta a perdu 60-70% du ciblage précis → le créatif EST le ciblage
- TikTok recommande de rafraîchir les créatifs toutes les 7-14 jours
- ROAS = Revenus générés ÷ Budget pub dépensé (ex: 4 000€ revenus / 1 000€ pub = ROAS 4)

## 4. First Call Script (15 min)

### Pre-Call
- [ ] Review their last 5 posts
- [ ] Check their website / product
- [ ] Estimate their ad budget (3k-20k€/mo)
- [ ] Note: What are they scaling right now?

### During Call

**[0–2 min] Warm-up**
```
"Thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy.
I've been following your [platform] strategy—really smart.
Why'd you go all-in on [platform] right now?"

[Listen. Let them talk.]
```

**[2–6 min] Diagnosis (Listen 80%, talk 20%)**
```
"What's your biggest creative bottleneck right now?"

[Wait for answer]

"How often do you need new variations?"

[Wait for answer]

"What happens when a creative underperforms—do you iterate fast or move on?"

[Wait for answer]

"Who handles your creative now—freelancer, in-house, you?"

[Wait for answer]
```

**[6–12 min] Pitch & Offer**
```
"Here's what I do: I help founders test 5 creative angles in 48h 
instead of waiting weeks. Each angle has a different hook—
agitation, aspiration, social proof, urgency, curiosity.

You run them for a week, see which one's winning, 
then scale that angle hard.

The reason I'm calling now: 
I'm looking for 2–3 beta testers at 500€ 
(normally 1,200€) in exchange for a testimonial 
once we have results.

Would it make sense to do a test sprint with me? 
Zero risk—if it doesn't help, no hard feelings.
I'd deliver 5 variations in 48h, 
you run them for a week, 
and we hop on a call to review data together."

[Stop talking. Wait for response.]
```

**[12–15 min] Close**
```
IF YES: 
"Great. Let me send you a brief form. 
Once I get your info, we kick off tomorrow."

IF MAYBE: 
"What would make this a yes for you?"

IF NO: 
"No worries. Keep crushing it on [platform]."
```

## 6. Day 7 Debrief Call Script

### Pre-Call Prep (1 day before)
```
Subject: Ready for our debrief tomorrow? 

Hey [Client name],

Looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time].

Before we jump on, can you grab these metrics from your Ad Manager?
• Hook rate (how many watch 3+ seconds on each variation)
• CTR (click-through rate per ad)
• CPA (cost per acquisition if tracking conversions)

Have them ready—we'll read them together.

See you then!
```

### During Debrief Call (20 min)
```
[Pull up their Ad Manager together via screen share]

"OK, let's read this. Show me your metrics."

[They share screen or paste numbers]

"So here's what I'm seeing:

[Hook A] crushed on hook rate (50%+ watch 3s) but lower CPA.
[Hook B] held best watch time throughout.
[Hook C] converted best (lowest CPA).
[Hook D] struggled across the board.
[Hook E] surprised—higher engagement than expected.

What jumps out at you?"

[Let them react. They'll naturally sell themselves.]

"So here's what I'd recommend:

Option 1 — Scale the winner:
Double down on [Hook C] for next 2 weeks.
Spend more budget on that angle, test different creatives *within* that hook.

Option 2 — Iterate faster:
We keep testing. Next sprint = 5 fresh angles.
Why? Because iteration beats optimization when you're early.

What's your gut telling you?"

[Wait for response]

"If you want to keep testing, I can do this 2x a month.
I'd deliver 2 sprints + 2 debriefs monthly for 2,500€.
That's 1 sprint every 2 weeks—fast iteration, always testing something new.

Interested in retainer?"

[Close.]
```

## 10. Pricing Decision Tree

Use this to price each prospect:

```
Question 1: Is this a launch client (first 2)?
   → YES: 500€ (launch price, in exchange for testimonial)
   → NO: Go to Q2

Question 2: Will they sign a retainer after 2 sprints?
   → Probably YES: 1,200€ first sprint (intro), 2,500€/mo retainer
   → Probably NO: 1,200€ (one-shot)
   → Don't know yet: 1,200€ (one-shot, reassess at debrief)

Question 3: Is the client already paying (retainer)?
   → YES: Offer upsell at sprint 3 (more frequency, higher price)
   → NO: Debrief call = moment to propose retainer

Simple rule: 
- Launch (first 2): 500€
- One-shot (clients 3+): 1,200€
- Retainer: 2,500€ base (up to 4,000€ if 4+ sprints/month)
```

## 11. Mistakes to Avoid (Checklist)

- [ ] **Posting inconsistently.** Batch content on Fridays. Set it and forget it.
- [ ] **Pitching too early.** Wait 3–4 comments before DM. Looks spammy otherwise.
- [ ] **DM is too long.** Keep first message under 50 words. Hook curiosity, save details for call.
- [ ] **Following random people.** Focus on target niche only (e-commerce, SaaS founders).
- [ ] **Talking about YOUR service.** Always lead with their problem: "I see you're scaling..."
- [ ] **No CRM tracking.** Use a spreadsheet. You'll lose track otherwise.
- [ ] **Skipping the Day 7 debrief.** This is your #1 moment to convert to retainer.
- [ ] **Accepting any price negotiation.** 500€ is launch price, 1,200€ is standard. Don't go lower.
- [ ] **Not collecting testimonials.** Every client = ask for testimonial once sprint works.
- [ ] **Feeling rushed.** Month 1 is content. No client pressure. Stay consistent.
