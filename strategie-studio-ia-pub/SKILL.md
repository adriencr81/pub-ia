# Stratégie Studio IA Pub

## Overview

This skill helps you design and execute a complete strategy for an AI-powered creative testing studio serving e-commerce and SaaS founders.

The core insight: **You're not selling videos. You're selling a testing system** — analysis + hypothesis + protocol + data review. This positioning unlocks pricing power and sustainable differentiation.

---

## Core Business Model

### What You're Selling
- 5 AI-generated video variations in 48h
- Competitive analysis (10 active ads in the niche)
- Hypothesis document (why each hook, which pain it addresses)
- Testing protocol (budget, duration, KPI to track)
- Data debrief at Day 7

**Not:** beautiful content. **But:** speed of iteration and risk reduction.

### Key Numbers
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Seuil rentabilité | Mois 2–3 (2 clients one-shot) |
| Marge estimée | 75–80% |
| Coûts fixes/mois | 150–200€ (outils) |
| Objectif Y1 | 45–55k€ CA, 4–6 retainers actifs |

```

├─ Taille : 10–100 employees (e-commerce ou SaaS)
├─ Budget pub : 3k–20k€/mois sur Meta ou TikTok
├─ Douleur : Créas épuisées toutes les 2 semaines
│   └─ Pas d'équipe créa interne

└─ Attitude : Pragmatique. Résultats > outil

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Budget < 2k€/mois (trop petit pour avoir besoin de test structure)
- Pas de scaling actif sur Meta/TikTok (pas de pain point)
- Agences classiques ou studios internes (ton service les rend obsolètes)

## Service Tiers

Use this three-level structure to create clear pricing ladders and match client maturity.

### Tier 1: Sprint Lancement (500€, one-shot)
**Target:** First 2–3 clients (with testimonial in exchange)

**What's included:**
- 5 AI video variations
- Competitive analysis (10 competitor ads)
- Hypothesis document
- Testing protocol
- NO debrief

**Why this tier:** Lower barrier to entry. You use these as case studies / testimonials for pricing power later.

### Tier 2: Sprint Standard (1,200€, one-shot)
**Target:** 4th+ client onward

**What's included:**
- Everything in Tier 1 PLUS
- Day 7 debrief (20 min call)

**Conversion moment:** During this debrief, you propose the retainer. The call is where you read data together and the client realizes they want to repeat.

### Tier 3: Retainer (2,500–3,500€/month)
**Target:** Clients actively scaling

**What's included:**
- 2 sprints per month
- Day 7 debrief after each sprint
- Direct access (faster responses, priority)

**Expansion path:** As client grows, offer 3–4 sprints/month at 3,500–4,500€.

## Service Delivery Workflow

Each sprint follows this 8–10h structure. **Consistency matters more than perfection.**

| Step | Duration | Tools | Output |
|------|----------|-------|--------|
| Client brief | 1h | Video call + form | Brand assets, product link |
| Competitive analysis | 2h | Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, Minea | Doc: 10 competitor ads + hooks |
| Script writing | 1h | Claude | 5 hooks, 5 scripts (60–90s each) |
| Video production | 3–4h | HeyGen or Arcads | 5 uncut variations |
| Final edit | 1h | CapCut | Subtitles, music, watermark, exports |
| Delivery + doc | 30 min | Notion / WeTransfer | Client folder + hypothesis doc |

**Critical rule:** Never skip the hypothesis document. It's what separates "cheap video tool" from "strategic partner." 1 page explaining *why* each hook, *what pain* it addresses, *which segment* it targets.

## Positioning & Messaging

### The Core Pitch
> "I help e-commerce founders testing on Meta and TikTok to test 5 creative angles in 48h — for 1/3 the cost of an agency and 5x faster risk-mitigation than testing one angle at a time."

### What NOT to Say
❌ "I make AI videos" — You're selling testing infrastructure, not a video tool.

❌ "Cheaper than agencies" — Reframes as cost-cutting. Say: "You divide creative risk by 5 for 1/3 the price."

❌ "Guaranteed results" — Never. You deliver testable creatives and methodology, not ROAS. Be explicit in contracts.

### Right Framing
✅ Position as **strategic partner**, not vendor.
✅ Always connect to data: "We reduce which angles you burn budget on."
✅ Emphasize speed: "48h vs 2 weeks from freelancers means iterate faster."

## Client Acquisition Strategy

### Channel: LinkedIn Organic + Warm Outreach

**Why LinkedIn:** Founder-to-founder, decision-makers, no algorithm chaos like TikTok.

#### Content Cadence (3 posts/week)
1. **Monday — Ad Breakdown** (most shareable)
   - Analyze 1 live e-commerce ad on Meta or TikTok
   - Dissect: hook, story structure, psychological trigger
   - Format: short video or carousel

2. **Wednesday — Making-Of / Process**
   - Behind-the-scenes of a test sprint
   - Show the hypothesis document, the scripts, uncut footage
   - Transparency = trust-building

3. **Friday — Opinion / Hot Take**
   - Contrarian stance on e-commerce ad dogma
   - "Why most creators get hook strategy wrong"
   - Generates comments, engagement, reach

**Timeline:** 8 weeks of consistent posting before expecting conversions. Post before clients exist — content builds credibility.

#### Warm Outreach Flow (4-week cycle)
```
Week 1–3: Presence
├─ Identify 20 founder targets on LinkedIn
├─ Comment substantively on 5 of their posts/week
└─ NO selling, only value-add

Week 4: Contact
├─ DM after becoming a familiar face
├─ "I'm testing a service for founders scaling on Meta—
│   interested in 48h test sprint at launch price (500€) 
│   + your testimonial?"
└─ Keep it short and benefits-focused
```

**Message template:**
```
Hey [Name],

I've been following your growth on Meta and love the creative 
direction. I'm building a test system for founders like you—
5 ad variations in 48h with analysis, not just videos.

I'm looking for 2 beta clients at 500€ in exchange for a 
testimonial. Would this be useful for what you're running?

Happy to do a 15-min call if it resonates.

## Retention & Expansion Mechanics

### The Day 7 Debrief is Your Closing Call

After 7 days of client testing their 5 variations, schedule a 20-min call. This is where conversion happens.

**During the debrief:**
- Read data together in real-time
- Identify which hook won, why
- Discuss what to test next

**The pitch (if data is good):**
> "This worked. You'll want to iterate 2x a month, right? At retainer, you get 2 sprints monthly + this call every time, plus direct access. It's 2,500/month for active scaling."

**The pitch (if data is unclear):**
> "The numbers are mixed. That's exactly why we do this—we just learned which angle *doesn't* work. Next sprint, we pivot. Ready to book it?"

**Why it works:** Client sees the system in action. They realize they want to repeat. You're not selling—you're proposing the next sprint as obvious.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Tools commoditize (Creatify improves) | High | Medium | Move upmarket: strategy + data > production. Unbundle analysis. |
| Client blames you for poor ROAS | Medium | High | Clear contract: you deliver testable creatives. Targeting/offer/landing page = client responsibility. Educate in brief. |
| Inconsistent content output | High | Medium | Batch posts (3 in 1 session/week). Fixed editorial calendar. Time-block it. |
| Scale bottleneck (4–5 clients max solo) | Low | Medium | Bring in assistant or subcontract production at M6+ if opportunity justifies. |
| AI avatars look fake | Medium | Low | Use most lifelike avatars (HeyGen Ultra, Arcads top tiers). Reframe as advantage: "Authentic is overrated; tested wins." |

## 4 Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **Content before clients.** Post for 8 weeks before pitching. Credibility first.
2. **Day 7 debrief is the sale.** Never skip it. This is where retainer conversions happen.
3. **Cover yourself on ROAS.** Contract is clear: you deliver testable creatives, not guaranteed results.
4. **Never raise prices on existing clients.** Launch pricing (500€) stays launch pricing. Growth comes from new clients at full price.
